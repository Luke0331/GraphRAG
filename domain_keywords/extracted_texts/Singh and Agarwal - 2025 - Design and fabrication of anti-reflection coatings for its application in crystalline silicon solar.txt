Vol.:(0123456789) https://doi.org/10.1007/s10854-025-14809-9 J Mater Sci: Mater Electron (2025) 36:721 Design and fabrication of antireflection coatings for its application in crystalline silicon solar cells Anterdipan Singh1 and Pratima Agarwal1,* 1 Department of Physics, Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, Assam 781039, India ABSTRACT In this study, Single Layer Anti-reflection Coatings (SLARC) of ITO, SiO2, and TiO2, alongside with Double Layer Anti-reflection Coating (DLARC) made of SiO2/TiO2, were designed using simulation studies and subsequently fabricated on silicon substrates using RF sputtering techniques. Prior to design the ARC coatings, a method to determine the refractive index of single layer thin films deposited on silicon substrates, utilizing experimental reflectance data were devised and later used for ARC designing purpose. For refractive index calculation, this method relies on two steps: the first step includes the calculation of extinction coefficient values from the reflection and transmission spectra of films on corning 1737 substrates and later using these calculated extinction coefficient values for fitting the reflectance data of the films on silicon substrate. The experimentally observed average reflectance (Ravg) (400 nm  Œª  1100 nm) was 10.95% for the ITO SLARC and 4.39% for the SiO2/TiO2 DLARC on silicon substrate. A strong correlation was observed between the theoretically predicted and experimentally measured reflectance spectra of the fabricated ARCs. Subsequently, these refractive indices (n) and extinction coefficient (k) values were used to simulate the performance silicon solar cells under different ARC conditions using TCAD Sentaurus. The analysis of different performance parameters includes open-circuit voltage (VOC), short-circuit current density (JSC), reflectance, external quantum efficiency (EQE), and photovoltaic efficiency. The results indicated the superiority of DLARC (SiO2/TiO2) based devices which achieved a maximum efficiency of 20.35%, compared to 18.40% for ITO SLARC and 13.54% for devices without any ARC. 1 Introduction Crystalline Silicon-based solar cells have gained sig- nificant attention as an excellent and cost-effective means for harnessing solar energy. Its domination in the market is due to the high abundance and mature manufacturing technologies with good power con- version efficiencies. However, the reflection losses from the Silicon-based solar cells severely cripple the device efficiency. Various ARCs have been optimized theoretically and experimentally to reduce the optical losses in silicon solar cells. [311] Interference-type ARCs especially Single Layer ARC and Double Layer ARC are the most commonly used type in Solar cells. These ARC coatings reduce reflection by the concept of phase changes in light and the dependence of the reflectivity on the refractive index [12]. Among the materials studied, oxides such as SiO2, MgO, AZO, SnO2, and TiO2 have been thoroughly investigated and optimized for ARC applications in silicon-based solar cells [13, 14]. SiO2 and TiO2 are known for their excellent stability, mechanical hardness, and minimal absorption in the 400 nm-1100 nm wavelength range, owing to their wide bandgap nature. Indium Tin Oxide (ITO) is another widely used transparent conducting oxide as electrode in solar cells due to its high optical band gap (3.54.3 eV), high optical transmissivity ( 85%), low sheet resistance, and stability [15]. Consequently, ITO, TiO2, and SiO2 were selected for fabricating ARCs on silicon substrates using the RF sputtering technique. The optical characteristics of these filmssuch as thickness, absorption coefficient, and refractive indexplays a crucial role in determining their performance and functionality as an ARC in the silicon-based solar cells. Therefore, it is necessary to comprehend and precisely measure these optical parameters to maximize the effectiveness in devices. Widely used methods, such as the Swanepoel method or Transfer-matrix method (TMM) [1618], rely on the presence of interference fringes in transmission spectra or reflection spectra [19]. Interference fringes typically occur only in films with sufficient thickness, which poses a significant limitation when working with the thin films commonly used in solar cell ARC application or emitter layer applications for silicon heterojunction solar cells. The thicknesses required for ARC application or emitter layer application in solar cells are usually significantly lower than the one required for yielding interference fringes. This constraint can result in inaccuracies in designing and fabricating anti-reflection coatings (ARCs) and in determining the refractive index of new materials for silicon heterojunction solar cells. To address this issue, we have developed a new method for determining the refractive index of films deposited on non-transparent substrates, such as silicon. Unlike widely used methods, our approach can work without the need of interference fringes, making it suitable for designing ARCs and determining the refractive index of new materials. The experimentally obtained reflectance spectra of ITO, TiO2 and SiO2 ARC films deposited on silicon substrates were analyzed and fitted against our theoretical proposed reflectance model to obtain the optical constants of the films. The optical parameters obtained through our proposed method showed strong agreement with previously published data. Subsequently, Single Layer ARC (SLARC) of ITO, TiO2, SiO2 and SiO2-TiO2 Double Layer ARC (DLARC) were designed theoretically and then fabricated on silicon substrates. The experimentally measured reflectance spectra of the fabricated ARCs closely match with the theoretical predictions. Notably, the SiO2TiO2 DLARC demonstrated a significant reduction in average reflectance (Ravg = 4.39%) compared to ITO (Ravg = 10.95%), TiO2 (Ravg = 14.97%), and SiO2 (Ravg = 16.07%) SLARCs within the 400nm-1100nm wavelength range. Using the derived refractive index and extinction coefficient values alongside the measured ARC layer thicknesses, influence of ITO SLARC and SiO2-TiO2 DLARC on the performance parameters such as the short-circuit current density (JSC), open-circuit voltage (VOC), External quantum efficiency (EQE), and conversion efficiency of the silicon-based solar cells were studied using TCAD sentaurus.
